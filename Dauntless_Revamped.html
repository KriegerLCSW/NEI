Context

George is a resident of Earth-616. When Peter Krieger made his debut as Spider-Man, George was already in his 40's. Becoming a superhero was the last thing on his mind, but fate had other ideas...

History

You know the story... Everyone knows the story. Peter Krieger was a sickly teenager, orphaned and living with his aunt and uncle. A bright kid, he was hopeless socially, and bullied mercilessly. That is, until he was bitten by a radioactive spider, and gained the proportional strength, stamina, agility, and near-precognitive "Spider-Sense" of a common house spider. That story, however, is not this story...

George Krieger was not sickly, nor a teenager, nor orphaned. He grew up in a tumultuous, emotionally abusive home, and struggled to find his way, but he did do just that. He attended college, earning a Bachelor's degree in psychology, followed by Master's degrees in social work and education. He raised a daughter out of wedlock, and then married when he was 41, having a son and another daughter. After working as a social worker and psychotherapist in nearly every arena, he finally settled down as a school social worker in an elementary school, just a few miles outside the Chicago city limits. That is where our story begins.

Peter Krieger was bitten by a Parasteatoda tepidariorum, or common house spider, and gained proportionate strength, stamina, speed, agility, and a precognitive "Spider Sense".

Acting as a chaperone when the first-graders at his school went on a field trip to Chicago's Museum of Science and Industry, George was bitten by a radioactive spider, but of a different genus and species. Bitten by a Phidippus audax, or bold jumping spider, that had stowed away on the `U-505 Submarine, George gained a similar power-set to Peter and other Spider-Men and -Women, including enhanced (in comparison to other Spiders) strength, as well as enhanced vision and "Spider-Sense".

Powers and Abilities

Dauntless shares the same basic power set as Spider-Man from Earth-616. He has superhuman strength, stamina, speed, and agility. Since he was bitten as an adult, and by a different species of spider, he is physically larger and stronger than his Spider-Counterparts, and if the others surpass him in any given area, it is hard to detect. The upper limits of his strength approach Class 100 strength. While he can go toe-to-toe with beings who are much stronger, he has his limits. While the upper limit of his 12 strength suggests that he can lift up to 104 tons, the general benchmark of 60 tons is far more realistic. The 12 strength primarily allows him to have a Cling of 11, giving him the ability to carry over 50 tons while wall-crawling. To that end, he is able to remain adhered to a vertical surface, or even upside down, while holding a Boeing 737. His reflexes have been described as 40 times as fast as a normal human, and while not invulnerable, he can withstand a tremendous amount of physical punishment. His tendons have the tensile strength of spring steel, and a standing jump can carry him nearly 1 mile straight up, and 2 miles vertically

Bold Jumping Spiders do not build webs, but George knows a good idea when he sees it. While George lacks the Gadgetry skill, Peter was more than happy to share the tech with him.

His coordination is enhanced to superhuman levels as well. Although his haphazard impromptu gymnastic style looks awkward at times, he can even dodge a bullet from a distance of 40 feet, reacting after it has been fired. He can achieve a state of equilibrium in even the most precarious positions.

Even more remarkable, he has developed what is known as his “Spider Sense”. This is a nearly precognitive ability to know when he is in danger. This is not limited to only physical danger. It will even warn him if someone is watching him change into his costume (but only if said person does not already know his secret ID, apparently).

Multiple sources of extreme danger can, it seems, overload his “Spider-sense”. This causes so much mental noise as to prevent it from being of any use. Likewise, a single, cosmic threat such as the Beyonder may actually inflict enough mental Bashing damage to incapacitate him. All of those effects are more in the domain of plot device than game limitation, however.

All of George Krieger’s previous physical limitations such as weak knees, bad back, slow metabolism, etc. have been corrected by his transformation into Spider-Man.

Description

The classic red-and-blue and the alien black costume are illustrated here, as are the Electro-proof costume (black with brown padding) and the Spider-Armour (silvery metallic plates).

Personality

George is a great deal older than nearly all of the other Spiders. With age comes experience, and with experience comes wisdom. As a result, George is not impulsive like Peter and Miles. He is thoughtful, intelligent, knowledgable, and able to quickly assimilate and analyze large amouts of information. He is often the "voice of reason" in any group of Spiders. For example, during Across the Spider-Verse, George stood up to Miguel and publically opposed his plan to imprison Miles.

Pulling punches

Spider-Man pulls his punches all the time. He’s very careful not to accidentally inflict severe wounds with his superhuman strength. When so doing, he does not use the usual rules, but simply hits people with a lower EV than his maximum.

Against most human-level opponents, he will limit his EV to 05 ; he will usually rise to 07 against tough superhumans. When particularly angry or when it is obvious he can go all-out (invulnerable opponents, heavy armour…) he will use a full 09.

This does not constitute a Drawback, Limitation, Subplot, etc.. He’s just being careful.

Web of Spider-Man

WEBSHOOTERS [BODY 05, Flash: 08, Force shield: 06, Gliding: 01, Glue: 10, Projectile weapons: 05, Stretching: 03, R#02, Bonuses & Limitations :

  • All Powers have a Range equal to Stretching.
  • Flash is done by covering the target’s eyes with webbing (-1). It lasts until the webbing is disposed of (+1 – disposing of webbing is done just like for a Glue attack, see below).
  • Force shield takes one full Phase to activate (-1, no other Action during that Phase).
  • Gliding takes two full Phases to activate (-1, no other Action during those Phases).
  • Glue has the Hardening Bonus (see below). Glue can also be used with a full cartridge of web fluid (see below). Glue can have an Area of effect (1 AP) if desired, as well as the Swinglines bonus (as described for the Snare Power).
  • Stretching has No Fine Manipulation (-2), cannot be used defensively (-1) and cannot carry straight physical attacks (-1).
  • All Powers saves for Glue, Flash and Stretching are Minor Marginal.
  • Webbing dissolves within about an hour (-0)].

Krieger carries three full reloads of web fluid in his utility belt ; usually reaching the R# indicates the webshooters are empty. It takes three Phases to have a new web fluid cartridge ready.

Esoteric yet not uncommon uses for the webshooters :

  • Force Shield power include actual web shields.
  • Web “mittens” to handle dangerous objects.
  • Cushions, nets or trampolines to catch falling objects and people.
  • Parachutes made out of webbing ; this is modelled by Gliding.
  • Structures like rafts or skis have been created out of webbing – treat it as a power stunt on Gliding.

Projectile weapons represent a strong squirt of web with a dry (and thus hardened) tip. This is not used very often. Likewise, Stretching is mostly used to grab weapons and other objects out of his foes’ reach.

To restrain exceptionally powerful opponents, Spider-Man will sometimes Glue them using an entire cartridge of web fluid (thus automatically meeting the R# and having to recharge). The Glue attack is conducted normally ; if it achieves positive RAPs, 4 more RAPs are instantly added to the existing RAPs.

The Hardening bonus for Glue indicates that the medium used to Glue becomes more efficient past the first Phase. If an opponent is Glued (ie, RAPs are scored by the Glue attempt) during one full Phase, automatically add the APs of the Glue power as supplementary RAPs.

For instance, if Spider-Man successfully webs somebody with 5 RAPs, on the next Phase and following the opponent will have to struggle against 15 RAPs with his STR/STR. This is a +2FC Bonus.
